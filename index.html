Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about pressure washing? Find quick answers to common questions here.

What is the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing uses high-velocity water spray (typically 2000-4000 PSI) to blast away dirt from hard surfaces like concrete. Soft washing uses low pressure combined with specialized, biodegradable cleaning solutions to safely kill mold, algae, and bacteria on delicate surfaces like shingles, stucco, and wood siding.

Will pressure washing damage my plants and landscaping?

No, we take extreme precautions to protect your gardens. We pre-wet and rinse all plant life before, during, and after the cleaning process, and use safe, eco-friendly, biodegradable solutions that won't harm your grass or landscaping.

How often should I clean my house siding and roof?

Generally, we recommend having your siding washed once a year to prevent algae and mold buildup. Roof cleaning is typically required every 2 to 3 years depending on local humidity and shade coverage.

Do I need to be home while you clean?

You do not need to be home for the service! We just ask that you close all windows and doors securely, clear your driveway of vehicles, and ensure your outdoor water spigot is turned on and accessible.
